10 Things Every Online Pet Store Owner Should Be Doing Right Now (But Probably Isn't)

📅 June 6, 2026 ✍️ PetStore.com Editorial Team 🏷️ Ecommerce · Marketing · Pet Industry

The numbers don't lie: Americans spent $158 billion on their pets in 2025, and that figure is forecast to hit $228 billion by 2031. But if you're running an independent online pet store, you've probably noticed that those billions aren't exactly flowing your way while Chewy posts $3.1 billion in quarterly sales. The good news? The same trends driving the industry's explosive growth are creating specific opportunities that Chewy and Amazon structurally cannot exploit.

1. Stop Fighting Chewy on Price. Start Out-Experting Them.

Chewy controls 42%+ of the online pet supply market. You will not beat them on price. Full stop.

What you can beat them on is expertise, curation, and community. A 2,000-word guide on "choosing a raw diet for a senior German Shepherd with joint issues" is worth more than any coupon code — it builds trust that converts browsers into buyers and turns buyers into lifetime customers.

Independent pet stores that win are the ones positioning themselves as the knowledgeable local expert who happens to have a world-class online store, not as a cheaper version of Chewy.

2. Build an Autoship Program — Your Customers Are Already On One (Just Not Yours)

Here's the scariest stat in pet ecommerce: 83% of Chewy's revenue comes from Autoship subscribers. That's not a feature. That's a moat.

If you sell consumables — food, treats, supplements, litter — and you don't have a subscription option, you're actively training your customers to go to Chewy for their repeat purchases. Apps like Recharge (for Shopify) let you spin up a "subscribe and save" program in a weekend. Offer 10-15% off on subscriptions, and you'll convert one-time buyers into reliable monthly revenue overnight.

3. Get Into the Niches That Chewy Treats Like an Afterthought

Raw food. Freeze-dried toppers. Functional calming chews. Eco-friendly tofu litter. Human-grade ingredients. Breed-specific formulas.

These categories are growing at 18-26% CAGR and the big retailers handle them poorly — bad merchandising, weak content, no expertise. That's your opening. Pet food launches globally grew 26% annually from 2021-2025, almost entirely driven by premium and functional products. A customer who found you because you carry the best freeze-dried quail topper in the country is not going to Amazon for their next order.

4. Own Long-Tail SEO Before Anyone Else Does

You cannot rank for "dog food." You can rank for "freeze-dried raw turkey dog food for senior dogs" — and that searcher has a credit card in their hand.

Google's AI Overviews are reshaping search results right now, and genuinely helpful, expert-authored content is winning. A pet store that publishes 50 well-researched, specific articles — breed guides, condition-specific nutrition advice, product comparisons — will own a library of organic traffic that compounds for years at zero marginal cost.

Start with your top 20 best-selling products and write the definitive guide for each one. That's it. That's the strategy.

5. TikTok Shop Is the Most Underused Channel in Pet Ecommerce

Gen Z is now 25% of U.S. pet owners and they make purchase decisions on TikTok and Instagram — not Google. And they spend.

TikTok Shop now compresses the entire funnel from discovery to checkout into a single scroll. Pet content is among the highest-engagement content on the platform — pet influencer posts see 10-40% engagement rates versus 1-3% for typical lifestyle accounts. A single viral video of a dog going crazy for your freeze-dried liver treats can sell out your inventory.

If you're not posting on TikTok and tagging your products, you're invisible to the fastest-growing buyer demographic in the industry.

6. Set Up These Three Klaviyo Flows (If You Do Nothing Else on This List)

The dominant stack for independent pet ecommerce right now is Shopify + Klaviyo, and for good reason. Over 117,000 brands use them together.

The three flows that pay for themselves immediately:

  • Abandoned cart — recovering even 10-15% of abandons is pure margin
  • Post-purchase upsell — someone bought a harness? Recommend the matching leash and a training treat sampler
  • Win-back sequence — if a customer hasn't ordered in 90 days, a targeted "we miss your pup" email with a time-limited discount is consistently the highest-ROI email you can send

This isn't advanced marketing. This is table stakes in 2026. If you don't have these running, start today.

7. Build a Loyalty Program Around Your Best Customers

Customer acquisition costs are rising across every channel. The math is brutal: acquiring a new customer costs 5-7x more than retaining an existing one.

A points program tied to your email flows — Smile.io or LoyaltyLion both integrate natively with Shopify + Klaviyo — creates a compounding retention engine. VIP tiers (early access to new products, free samples for top spenders, birthday treats for their pets) create the kind of emotional connection that Chewy's algorithm literally cannot replicate.

Your best 20% of customers are probably generating 60-70% of your revenue. Build something that makes them feel like insiders, not just order numbers.

8. Pet Humanization Is Your Tailwind — Position Into It

The single biggest macro force in the industry right now: pet owners increasingly treat their animals as family members, and they're spending accordingly.

Gen X — the highest-income pet owner segment, up 12% in ownership in 2025 — is driving demand for premium nutrition, preventive health products, and wellness supplements. These are people who research ingredients, read labels, and will pay a significant premium for quality they trust.

Position your store around premium, wellness-focused, expert-curated products. "We only carry brands we'd feed our own dogs" is a brand story that resonates — and it's one Amazon can never credibly tell.

9. Fix Your Shipping Strategy Before It Kills Your Margins

Heavy, bulky pet products make shipping cost management existential. Free shipping is now a customer expectation — but absorbing it on a 30-pound bag of kibble at a 20% margin is a fast path to unprofitability.

Some options that actually work:

  • Free shipping threshold — set it just above your average order value to pull up basket size
  • Flat-rate shipping subscriptions — a $9/month "free shipping club" converts well and locks in repeat behavior
  • Autoship ships free — using free shipping as the autoship incentive kills two birds with one stone

Build your shipping math into your product pricing before you launch anything new. The worst time to discover you're losing $6 per order is after 500 orders.

10. The Pet Tech Wave Is Coming — Get There First

The global pet tech market is projected to grow from $19.1 billion in 2026 to $52.9 billion by 2035. Smart feeders, GPS trackers, wearable health monitors, AI-powered vet consult tools — these categories barely exist in most independent pet stores.

You don't need to stock 50 pet tech SKUs. You need to be the store that your customers associate with knowing what's new and worth buying. Carry 3-5 well-chosen smart devices, write the honest "is this worth it?" review content, and you'll capture the curious early adopters who become your most enthusiastic evangelists.

The Bottom Line

The pet industry's growth isn't going anywhere. What's shifting is where that money goes — and the stores that win over the next five years will be the ones that out-expert, out-connect, and out-retain the big platforms rather than trying to out-price them.

Pick two items from this list. Build them out properly. Then come back for the next two.

Your customers love their pets more than they love Chewy's free shipping. Give them a reason to love your store, and they'll stay.
