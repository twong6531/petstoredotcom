59% of Your Customers' Dogs Are Overweight — Here's the $4.9 Billion Weight Management Category You're Missing

📅 July 24, 2026 ✍️ PetStore.com Editorial Team 🏷️ Wellness · Nutrition · Pet Obesity

Nearly 3 in 5 dogs and 61% of cats seen by U.S. vets are classified as overweight or obese — and 32% of their owners have no idea. They think their pet looks normal. The pet obesity management market was worth $2.1 billion in 2025 and is on track to reach $4.9 billion by 2034 at a 9.6% CAGR. Most independent pet stores are capturing essentially none of it. Here's how to change that.

The Fat Pet Epidemic Is Already in Your Customer Base

The Association for Pet Obesity Prevention has tracked this for over a decade, and the numbers keep moving in the wrong direction: 59% of dogs and 61% of cats evaluated by veterinary professionals are overweight or obese. In the strictly "obese" category — body condition scores of 8 or 9 out of 9 — it's 22% of dogs and 33% of cats.

Obesity doesn't just make a dog look chubby. It cuts a dog's lifespan by up to 2.5 years and dramatically raises the risk of Type 2 diabetes, osteoarthritis, kidney disease, hypertension, and respiratory disease. The same diseases. The same outcomes. Just in a dog who can't tell you they're struggling to breathe when they walk to the mailbox.

Your customers already feel this problem. They see their dog hesitating at the base of the stairs. They're getting the weight talk at their annual vet visit. They're googling "best food for overweight dogs" at 10pm. The question is whether those searches lead to your store — or to Chewy and Amazon, who have no particular reason to care whether the product actually works.

That's the opening. Let's walk through every part of it.

Weight Management Food — The Brands You Can Actually Win With

Prescription weight management diets — Hill's Metabolic, Royal Canin Satiety Support, Purina Pro Plan OM — require veterinary authorization to dispense. You're not going to beat the vet clinic at that game, and you shouldn't try.

The real opportunity is the over-the-counter weight management segment, which is growing fast precisely because most owners want to address their pet's weight before it becomes a prescription-level problem. The brands worth stocking:

  • Wellness CORE Reduced Fat — High-protein, lower-fat formula with a genuinely clean ingredient label. MSRP around $65 for a 24-lb bag. The most recommended option by holistic-leaning vets who want to avoid prescription channels.
  • Merrick Healthy Weight — Grain-inclusive (important for the sizable segment of owners who've moved back from grain-free), limited ingredient approach. Works well for the "weight concern plus food sensitivity" customer.
  • Nulo Freestyle Trim — Cod and tuna-based novel protein with a low glycemic index formula. Strong seller in the premium independent channel and distinctive enough to differentiate from big-box options.
  • Orijen Six Fish — Naturally very low carbohydrate, biologically appropriate protein ratios. Appeals to raw-adjacent customers who want serious weight management without the freezer logistics.

The content play here is easy and high-converting: a guide titled "5 weight management dog foods that are actually high in protein" beats any generic product page. Customers searching for this have a pet with a real problem and a credit card ready. Meet them with expertise, not a grid of thumbnails.

Smart Feeders: The $100–$300 Hardware Play With Real Margins

Overfeeding is the single most common driver of pet obesity, and the smart feeder category has reached a quality inflection point in 2026 where the products actually deliver on their promises. Margin profile: 40–55% at MAP, compared to the 18–25% you're earning on a bag of kibble.

The three worth carrying:

  • PETLIBRO Granary WiFi (~$100) — Offers 1–48 adjustable portion sizes, 5GHz app control, and a 5-liter hopper. Best entry-level option for owners who want control without overthinking it.
  • PETLIBRO Granary Camera (~$100) — Same feeder, with a 1080p night-vision camera and two-way audio added. The camera + feeder bundle is now a top seller because it addresses separation anxiety at the same time — one product, two pain points.
  • Whisker Feeder-Robot ($299) — The precision choice. Uses an actual built-in scale to measure food by weight rather than volume, eliminating portion drift. If a vet has put a pet on a structured weight loss regimen with specific gram targets, this is the only feeder that delivers on that accurately.

The bundle pitch writes itself: pair any weight management food with a smart feeder and call it a "Healthy Weight Starter Kit." Price it at $149–$189 depending on the feeder tier. You've just created a category, not just a product page — and a reason to reach out to every customer who's ever bought a bag of kibble from you.

Weight Management Supplements: The Biggest Slice of the Market

Food supplements account for 62% of the pet obesity management market by revenue — by a wide margin, the largest category segment. That tracks with human behavior: supplements are the first thing people reach for when they're worried but not yet ready to overhaul the whole diet. The same instinct plays out with pet owners.

The products worth adding to your weight wellness shelf:

  • Zesty Paws Vet Strength Weight Management — H&H Group launched this in March 2025 specifically targeting canine obesity with a formula that supports fat metabolism, digestion, and satiety. Has the brand recognition to self-explain to new customers.
  • VetriScience Vetri-Lean — Clinically studied formula featuring HMB (beta-hydroxy beta-methylbutyrate), which helps maintain lean muscle mass during caloric restriction. This matters because naive calorie reduction often causes muscle loss alongside fat loss — owners who know this detail will specifically seek this product out.
  • Native Pet Collagen — Not a weight supplement per se, but a natural companion purchase: overweight pets have elevated joint stress, which reduces activity, which accelerates weight gain. Collagen addresses the joint side of the equation. Position it as the "active recovery" partner to a weight management program.

Merchandising tip that moves product: place weight management supplements adjacent to joint support products on your site. The overlap is not coincidental — overweight pets have joint pain, joint pain prevents exercise, and the owner who's addressing one is almost certainly dealing with the other.

The GLP-1 Conversation Has Already Started — Get Ahead of It

In July 2026, CNBC ran the headline "Ozempic for cats? GLP-1 weight-loss drugs are coming for your fat pet." Two companies — Akston Biosciences and OKAVA Pharmaceuticals — are in active clinical trials for pet-specific GLP-1 therapies. Cornell University is recruiting overweight cats for a study of once-weekly injections. A Fox News piece on a six-month GLP-1 implant for pets went viral in pet owner Facebook groups within hours of publication.

Here's what this media moment means for your store: none of these products are approved yet. The human GLP-1 drugs (semaglutide, tirzepatide) cost $900–$1,350 per month in human doses — prohibitively expensive for the vast majority of pet budgets, even if a vet would prescribe them off-label. Most of your customers are going to read the headline, ask their vet, and hear "not yet" or "too expensive."

Then they need somewhere to go. That somewhere should be your store, with a content piece that explains the GLP-1 landscape honestly, explains why good nutrition + portion control + targeted supplements is the evidence-backed alternative right now, and walks them to your weight management collection. A blog post titled "What pet owners should know about GLP-1 drugs for dogs and cats" will rank well, get shared, and convert readers into buyers who trust you because you told them the truth.

How to Have the Weight Conversation Without Alienating Anyone

The tricky part: calling someone's dog fat is personal. Handle it clumsily and you lose the sale and the customer. Handle it right and you become the store they tell their friends about.

The framing that works:

  • Lead with health outcomes, not weight. "Support joint health and sustained energy as your pet ages" lands better than "for overweight dogs." Same product, same customer, zero defensiveness.
  • Use "ideal body condition" language. It's the clinical standard vets use, it's neutral, and it signals expertise without judgment.
  • Position it as proactive, not reactive. "Owners who start a weight management program before joint issues develop see dramatically better outcomes" is empowering. It makes the customer feel smart for acting early, not ashamed for having waited.
  • Build a self-serve resource hub. A "Healthy Weight" category page with a free downloadable body condition score guide, a FAQ on pet weight management food, and product recommendations creates value before asking for a purchase — and it runs 24/7 with no staff required.

The email sequence that converts: a 5-part "Healthy Weight Challenge" that walks owners through a body condition assessment in week one, food adjustment recommendations in week two, feeder setup in week three, and supplement introduction in week four — with relevant product links embedded naturally at each step. Klaviyo makes this straightforward to build. The owners who complete it will have bought three separate product categories by the end, and they'll feel like you coached them through a real outcome for their pet.

Build a "Healthy Weight Program" That Lives on Autoship

One-time bag of diet food is not the play here. The opportunity in weight management is an ongoing relationship with measurable outcomes — which is exactly the kind of relationship that converts to recurring autoship revenue.

A structured program that stacks naturally:

  • Month 1: Weight management food (8-week supply) + smart feeder. One-time hardware purchase establishes the infrastructure.
  • Month 2: Weight management supplement introduced on autoship. This is when the customer is seeing early results and is most motivated to maintain momentum.
  • Month 3+: Autoship renewal on food + supplement as a paired subscription. The combination becomes the routine.

At a $90/month average basket — food plus supplement, with a 15% subscribe-and-save discount — you're building customers worth over $1,000/year who are emotionally invested in their pet's progress and grateful you gave them a path forward that actually worked. Churn on this program is low because the stakes are high. Nobody cancels a subscription when their dog's life expectancy is on the line.

The pet obesity category is large, growing, underleveraged by independent stores, and desperately in need of expert guidance that the big platforms simply don't provide. That guidance is your competitive advantage. The products are the proof it works.

The Bottom Line

Sixty percent of the pets your customers own are carrying weight they shouldn't. Most of those customers don't know it. The ones who do know it aren't finding real solutions at most independent pet stores — because most stores haven't built the category.

Build a "Healthy Weight" collection. Stock two or three weight management food options, a smart feeder at two price points, and one or two targeted supplements. Create one piece of honest, expert content about how pet weight management actually works. Then put the consumables on autoship.

You'll have built something Amazon can't replicate: a real program with real outcomes for customers who needed a store that cared enough to take it seriously.
