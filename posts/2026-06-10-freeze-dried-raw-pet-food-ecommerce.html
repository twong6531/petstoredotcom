Freeze-Dried Raw Pet Food Is Dominating 2026 — Here's How to Turn It Into Your Highest-Margin Category

📅 June 10, 2026 ✍️ PetStore.com Editorial Team 🏷️ Nutrition · Ecommerce · Raw Feeding

At Global Pet Expo 2026, the same category dominated the New Products Showcase year after year: freeze-dried and air-dried raw formats, expanding beyond treats into complete diets, toppers, and hybrid products. Meanwhile, the global freeze-dried pet food market sits at $20.81 billion in 2026 and is headed for $33.67 billion by 2035. If your online store is still leading with kibble, you are watching one of the biggest margin opportunities in pet ecommerce pass you by.

Why Freeze-Dried Raw Is Having Its Moment Right Now

The raw feeding movement has been simmering for two decades, but it always had the same problem: convenience. Raw meat is perishable, messy, difficult to portion, and terrifying to ship. Freeze-dried and air-dried formats solve all of that at once.

Freeze-drying removes up to 98% of moisture at low temperatures, leaving a shelf-stable product that retains the protein density, enzyme activity, and nutrient profile of raw meat — with no refrigeration required and a dramatically reduced food safety footprint. Air-drying does something similar at slightly higher temperatures, yielding a denser, chewier texture that dogs find incredibly palatable.

The result: more than 29% of U.S. pet owners now prefer raw-inspired diets for their dogs and cats, and 61% prioritize ingredient transparency when buying pet food. Freeze-dried delivers on both counts in a format that actually works for ecommerce. Freeze-dried raw meal launches increased by 28% in 2024 alone, and that momentum has only accelerated into 2026.

The Ecommerce Advantage Nobody Talks About

Here's a detail that should get your attention if you're thinking about margins and logistics: freeze-dried pet food has an exceptional value-to-weight ratio.

A 14-oz bag of Stella & Chewy's freeze-dried raw dinner patties retails for $35–45 and ships in a small, lightweight package. Compare that to a 30-lb bag of kibble at $60–70 that costs $15–20 to ship and requires a pallet or oversized handling. Freeze-dried products are built for direct-to-consumer ecommerce in a way that traditional dry food simply isn't.

This matters enormously for your unit economics. Standard pet food margins run 25–35%. Premium freeze-dried and air-dried products consistently deliver gross margins of 45–55%, closer to what you'd see on supplements or accessories. And because customers are buying a $40 bag every 3–4 weeks rather than a $65 bag of kibble every 6 weeks, your average order frequency goes up too.

The Brands Worth Stocking Right Now

You don't need twenty SKUs to own this category. You need the right four or five brands that cover different customer entry points:

  • Stella & Chewy's — the category's flagship brand, founded after a rescue dog's health transformation on a raw diet. Their freeze-dried dinner patties and meal mixers are the most recognized raw format in independent pet retail. Start here.
  • Primal Pet Foods — raw since 2001, using organic produce and humanely raised meats. Strong brand trust among customers who've already done their research. Their freeze-dried nuggets come in an unusually wide protein variety (beef, duck, rabbit, venison, quail) that lets you serve the adventurous shopper.
  • The Honest Kitchen — human-grade, minimally processed, and USDA certified. If your customer base skews toward "ingredient label readers," this brand converts exceptionally well. Their dehydrated whole grain recipes are an easier on-ramp for customers making the transition from kibble.
  • Badlands Ranch — one of 2026's breakout brands, their Superfood Complete line leads with humanely raised beef, beef heart, and liver in an air-dried format. Strong online following and excellent packaging for social media content.
  • Dr. Marty — direct-response marketing juggernaut with a loyal customer base. Stock it and you'll get customers who are already sold on the brand actively searching for a convenient place to buy.

Complete Diets vs. Toppers: Lead With the Right Product

One of the most common mistakes pet store owners make when entering this category is leading with complete diets. That's the wrong move for most customers.

The raw feeding conversation is still a big mental leap for the average pet owner. "Switch your dog entirely to raw" is a hard sell. "Add a freeze-dried topper to whatever you're already feeding for a serious nutrition upgrade" is a very easy sell — and it's one your customers can say yes to without anxiety.

The smart approach is to merchandise toppers and meal mixers as your category entry point, and complete diets as the premium upgrade for customers who are already converted. Stella & Chewy's Meal Mixers, Primal's freeze-dried toppers, and The Honest Kitchen's food boosts all exist exactly for this strategy. They retail at $12–20, deliver high perceived value, and give a first-time raw customer a low-stakes, high-reward experience that makes the upsell to complete diets a natural next step.

Dog products dominate — 71% of freeze-dried raw purchases are for dogs — but don't neglect the cat side. Cat raw feeding is growing fast, it's deeply underserved in most independent stores, and a cat owner who finds a dedicated raw section specifically for their cat will remember you forever.

How to Talk About Safety Without Scaring Anyone Away

If you're going to sell freeze-dried raw, you need to know how to handle the safety conversation — because your customers will ask, and getting this wrong destroys trust.

The honest answer is nuanced: freeze-drying significantly reduces bacterial load (studies show freeze-dried samples consistently fall below acceptable bacterial thresholds where raw meat often doesn't), and the shelf-stable format removes the cross-contamination risks of handling wet raw. But freeze-drying is not cooking — it doesn't sterilize the way heat does.

For most healthy dogs and cats fed in households without immunocompromised individuals or young children, freeze-dried raw is a reasonable choice with manageable risk. The key safe-handling practices are simple: wash hands and bowls, don't leave rehydrated food sitting out, and store products as directed.

Don't oversell and don't over-warn. Just be direct. The brands who sell this category well — Stella & Chewy's publishes an excellent safe handling guide — frame it as a knowledgeable choice made by informed pet owners, not a reckless gamble. Lead with that framing and you'll convert the research-oriented customer who makes up the core of this market.

Build the SEO Case Before Your Competitors Do

Here's the SEO reality in 2026: the big box players are terrible at freeze-dried raw content. Chewy's product pages are thin. Amazon has no editorial voice. PetSmart's blog is an afterthought.

That means a dedicated independent store that publishes 8–10 genuinely useful pieces — "freeze-dried vs air-dried: what's the real difference," "how to transition your senior dog to a raw-inspired diet," "the best freeze-dried cat foods for picky eaters" — can rank on the first page for long-tail searches that have clear purchase intent and very little competition from authoritative sources.

This isn't a moonshot. It's a gap in the market that exists right now. Google's AI Overviews reward depth and expertise, not brand size. An independent store owner who actually knows this category can outrank Chewy on the searches that matter most.

Turn Freeze-Dried Buyers Into Your Best Autoship Customers

Customers who switch to freeze-dried raw don't go back to kibble. The repurchase rate in this category is among the highest in all of pet food — these owners are committed, they've seen results, and they need resupply on a predictable schedule.

That makes freeze-dried raw the single best category to drive into an autoship or subscription program. A customer who sets up a monthly delivery of their dog's Stella & Chewy's patties at a 15% discount has given you a relationship worth $400–600 a year — and they'll almost certainly add supplements, treats, and accessories to the same order over time.

The merchandising play: offer free shipping on autoship orders for freeze-dried raw (your margin supports it, and the lightweight products make it economical), and position "subscribe and save" as the default, obvious way to buy. Customers who are spending $40–50 per bag will jump at 15% off when they know they'll be buying it every month anyway.

The Window Is Open — But Not Forever

The freeze-dried raw category is at that perfect stage where demand is accelerating but the major platforms haven't figured out how to serve it well. That advantage belongs to independent specialty stores right now — stores that can curate the best brands, publish real expertise, and build the kind of community around raw feeding that no algorithm can replicate.

Stock the right five brands, write the definitive guides for your audience, lead with toppers as the entry point, and wire every purchase into your autoship program. That's the playbook. It works.
