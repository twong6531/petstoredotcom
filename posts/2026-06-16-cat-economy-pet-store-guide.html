Your Pet Store Is Built for Dog Owners. Here's Why That's Costing You Revenue in 2026.

📅 June 16, 2026 ✍️ PetStore.com Editorial Team 🏷️ Strategy · Cat Economy · Gen Z

Here's a stat most online pet store owners aren't tracking: cats now live in 53 million U.S. households — 39% of all homes — and cat ownership grew 5% year-over-year in 2025. For the first time in modern pet industry history, cat product sales are projected to outpace dog product sales across most categories over the next five years. The generation driving that surge — Gen Z — shops almost entirely online and spends like dog owners. If your store's product mix, navigation, email flows, and content are all optimized for dog households, you're structurally invisible to the fastest-growing buyer segment in the $165 billion pet industry. Here's exactly what to do about it.

The Cat Economy by the Numbers

The APPA's 2026 State of the Industry report makes it clear: cats are no longer the "other" pet category. U.S. pet industry expenditures hit $158 billion in 2025, growing to a projected $165 billion in 2026 — and within that, the cat-specific market is accelerating faster than the aggregate.

The data points that matter for store owners:

  • Cat ownership up 5% YoY — the largest single-year growth in over a decade
  • 38% of cat owners now purchase premium food, a 9-point jump from 2023
  • The global cat supplements market is growing at 7.2–7.8% CAGR through 2035
  • 54% of cat owners actively seek digestive health claims on products they buy
  • Online retail accounts for 63% of pet probiotic supplement purchases — cat owners already shop where you sell

Average cat owner spending is currently around $1,390 annually per household — still below dog owners — but that gap is closing as the premium segment expands and multi-cat households (the average is 1.7 cats per cat-owning home) normalize higher spend. A cat household on subscription for food, litter, and supplements easily hits $1,200–$1,600 a year in recurring revenue. Build enough of those relationships and you've built a serious business.

Who's Actually Driving the Surge — and What They Expect

The most important demographic finding in pet retail right now: Gen Z men are the fastest-growing cat owner segment in the country. Gen Z as a whole is adopting cats at higher rates than any previous generation did at the same age. The reasons are structural — smaller living spaces, more flexible lifestyles, urban environments — but the spending behavior matches that of devoted dog owners.

Gen Z pet owners discover products on TikTok, YouTube, and Instagram. 97% of Gen Z use social media as their primary source of shopping inspiration. They don't start on Google. They don't trust brand advertising. What they do trust: authentic reviews from real cat owners, before-and-after content showing actual results, and expert-written content that demonstrates genuine ingredient knowledge without talking down to them.

They also treat their cats with the same "pet humanization" intensity as older millennial dog owners — premium nutrition, preventive health supplements, enrichment products, and wellness-first purchasing decisions. The category word they respond to is wellness, not value. Price is secondary to trust and ingredient quality.

The Five Cat Product Categories You Should Be Stocking Now

Not all cat products are equally positioned for growth. Here are the categories with the strongest momentum and the specific brands that are performing:

1. High-Protein Wet Food
Wet food is the fastest-growing cat food format right now, and the premium tier is where independent stores have a real advantage. Weruva's BFF and Cats in the Kitchen lines are cult favorites with Gen Z cat owners. Tiki Pets and Wellness CORE Pate round out the top tier. Orijen Cat & Kitten — $90+ per bag, ingredient-obsessive buyers — should be a fixture in any serious cat catalog.

2. Raw and Freeze-Dried Formats
Viva Raw and Instinct Raw have built loyal cat owner followings for complete raw diets. Primal and Stella & Chewy's freeze-dried lines work as both complete meals and toppers — a versatile product that fits multiple buyer intents and autoship schedules.

3. Feline Functional Supplements
This is the highest-margin cat category in 2026. Digestive health is the lead concern: VetriScience Vetri Mega Probiotic (clinical-grade, vet-recommended) and Zesty Paws Advanced Probiotic Bites are both strong performers. PetHonesty Pre + Probiotic is gaining fast. Calming supplements (especially for apartment cats) and kidney/urinary health products round out the functional stack — all growing double-digits.

4. Enrichment and Puzzle Feeders
One of the most underserved categories in most stores. Licki Mats, Nina Ottosson puzzle feeders, and KONG Cat toys satisfy the Gen Z buyer's drive toward environmental enrichment and behavioral wellness. Margins are strong, they photograph brilliantly for social content, and they're consistent repeat purchases as cats master each difficulty level.

5. Sustainable Litter
Tofu litter is having a genuine breakout moment in 2026. Younger cat owners are treating litter as a sustainability decision — brands like PetFusion and ökocat are positioned perfectly. Flushable, compostable, plant-based formats are growing fast and have strong environmental messaging that resonates with Gen Z values. And litter is the ultimate repeat purchase — built for autoship.

Merchandise Cat Products Like They Matter — Because They Do

The structural problem in most online pet stores: "cat" is buried three clicks deep, after six sub-menus of dog food varieties. If 39% of U.S. households own cats, your store's navigation should reflect that proportion.

Practical changes that move the needle:

  • Dedicated "Cat Shop" top-level nav entry — not a dropdown option under "All Pets"
  • Cat-specific landing pages by need: "Digestive Health for Cats," "Raw Feeding for Cats," "Cat Enrichment" — these capture long-tail search traffic and convert better than generic category pages
  • Bundle pages built for cat households: a "New Cat Parent Starter Kit" (food + probiotic + puzzle feeder + litter) at a 12% bundle discount removes decision friction and drives higher average order value
  • Klaviyo species segmentation: if you're sending the same email about a new dog food launch to your cat owner segment, you're training them to ignore your emails. Tag every customer by species at checkout and segment from day one

These aren't advanced changes. They're the kind of basic merchandising respect that tells a cat owner "this store actually gets me" — and that feeling is worth more than any discount.

Content and Social Strategies That Convert Cat-Owning Gen Z Shoppers

The content that works for Gen Z cat buyers differs from what works for older dog owner audiences, both in format and platform.

For SEO, long-form expert comparisons perform exceptionally well: "Weruva vs. Tiki Pets vs. Wellness CORE: Which High-Protein Wet Cat Food Is Actually Worth It?" or "The 6 Best Probiotics for Cats With Sensitive Stomachs (Ranked by a Pet Nutrition Specialist)." These are deliberate, research-mode queries from people who are going to buy — they just need to trust the source giving them the answer. Be that source.

For social, TikTok cat content consistently outperforms dog content by engagement rate — the platform's audience skews younger and more urban, matching the Gen Z cat owner demographic exactly. Content formats that drive actual purchases:

  • "My three cats tried six different toppers — here's the brutal honest ranking" (real UGC-style, no polish)
  • Before-and-after coat or digestion improvement videos from probiotic customers
  • "What's in my cats' monthly haul" unboxing content that naturally showcases your product mix

You don't need a professional videographer. You need a phone, decent lighting, and a camera-friendly cat. The authenticity is the product.

Build Cat Autoship Revenue Before Your Competitors Wake Up

The highest-lifetime-value customers you can acquire in 2026 are cat households on subscription. Here's the math: a two-cat household on autoship for premium wet food, a probiotic, and litter is worth $100–$130 per month — $1,200–$1,560 annually — before any discretionary purchases. That's recurring revenue at the margin structure of a SaaS business, not a retail store.

Cat autoship works particularly well because:

  • Premium wet food is consumed on a predictable schedule (easy to set the right cadence)
  • Supplements require daily dosing — set-and-forget autoship is genuinely more convenient
  • Litter runs out on a reliable cycle — the biggest driver of "oh no, I need to order right now" anxiety

Build a cat-specific autoship track with targeted incentives: 15% off on recurring food and litter orders, free shipping always, and a quarterly "Cat Wellness Sample" box for active subscribers that introduces new supplement and treat SKUs. Market it with the pain point: "Never run out of litter at 11pm again." That's not just a value proposition — it's a genuine quality-of-life improvement for busy urban cat owners who are your core customer.

The window to own this customer before Chewy wins them to their autoship program is narrow. Cat owners who haven't yet committed to a subscription are still in play. Go get them.

The Bottom Line

The cat economy is not a niche trend — it's a structural shift in who owns pets in America and what they're willing to spend. Gen Z's cat ownership surge is bringing a digital-native, premium-oriented, wellness-focused buyer into the market who shops online, discovers on social, and wants to subscribe for the products they love.

Most online pet stores aren't ready for them. If you move now — on product mix, merchandising, content, and autoship — you'll own this customer before the category gets crowded. That's the entire playbook.
