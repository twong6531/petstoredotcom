A Bored Dog Destroys $400 in Furniture Per Year — The Enrichment Category Pet Stores Are Sleeping On

📅 July 2, 2026 ✍️ PetStore.com Editorial Team 🏷️ Strategy · Enrichment · Merchandising

Veterinary behaviorists have been saying it for years: mental exercise is just as important as physical exercise for dogs and cats. Pet owners are finally listening — and they're shopping for it. Lick mats. Puzzle feeders. Snuffle mats. Treat-dispensing wobble toys. These aren't fringe niche products anymore. According to WholesalePet's 2026 trend report, "enrichment has officially moved from niche to necessity." The global pet toys market hit $10.61 billion in 2025 and is projected to reach $22.42 billion by 2033 — with interactive and enrichment products leading the growth. If this category isn't a serious line item in your store right now, you're leaving margin on the table every single day.

Why Enrichment Is Having Its Moment in 2026

A few forces have converged to make enrichment products the category of the year for independent pet stores.

First, the behavioral science has gone mainstream. Boredom and understimulation are now widely understood as the root cause of common pet behavior problems — destructive chewing, excessive barking, anxiety, and weight gain from inactivity. Pet owners who previously bought toys occasionally are now actively building "enrichment routines" and shopping for tools to support them.

Second, post-pandemic dogs are struggling. Pets adopted during lockdown spent three years with their owners home all day. As work patterns normalized, separation anxiety and boredom-driven destruction spiked — and the internet is full of vet-recommended enrichment solutions. Searches for "lick mat" and "snuffle mat" have grown over 50% in search interest in the last two years.

Third, this category photographs and videos beautifully. A dog working through a puzzle feeder or going to town on a peanut butter lick mat is content gold. TikTok and Instagram have turned enrichment products into impulse buys millions of times over — and independent pet stores are perfectly positioned to capture that demand.

The Core Enrichment Categories to Stock

Don't try to carry everything at once. Start with these four proven product types that cover the majority of customer demand:

Lick mats are the gateway drug of enrichment. Spread peanut butter, plain Greek yogurt, wet food, or pumpkin purée on the textured surface and your dog or cat is occupied for 15–20 focused minutes. The global lick mat market was valued at $161 million in 2025 and is growing at roughly 15% CAGR — heading toward $800 million by 2033. Price range: $12–$28. Margins: 45–60% on quality silicone versions.

Snuffle mats engage a dog's nose — their primary sense — by hiding kibble or treats in fabric tufts that require foraging to find. A 10-minute snuffle session provides the mental equivalent of a 30-minute walk, according to veterinary behaviorists. Customers who buy snuffle mats almost always come back: they wear out and get replaced, or owners want different difficulty levels.

Puzzle feeders and slow feeders convert mealtime from a 30-second inhale to a 10-minute problem-solving exercise. The market for interactive dog toys and puzzle feeders is projected to grow at 13% CAGR from 2025 to 2033. They also address a real health problem: dogs that eat too fast are at elevated risk for bloat, which can be fatal in large breeds.

Treat-dispensing toys — the classic Kong-style wobble dispensers and more sophisticated rolling dispensers — are the entry point for customers who aren't yet sure about puzzle toys. These are easy to explain, broadly appealing, and convert readily from impulse to repeat purchase.

The Brands That Move Units

You don't need to curate 40 enrichment brands. You need the right three or four — and your customers will thank you for the editorial clarity.

KONG is the category anchor. Founded in 1976, it's the brand veterinarians recommend by default, and most households with a dog already own at least one. Stock the Classic in multiple sizes, the Wobbler, and the Gyro. KONG also opens conversations: a customer buying a KONG Classic is a perfect upsell target for lick mats, treat spreads, and stuffing recipes.

Nina Ottosson by Outward Hound is the gold standard for puzzle feeders. The line runs from Level 1 (beginner) to Level 4 (expert), giving you a natural upgrade path as customers return for more challenge. The Dog Tornado and Dog Brick are perennial Amazon best-sellers — carry them and you capture customers who want to try before they buy beyond mass-market.

LickiMat is the original and still the most trusted lick mat brand. Their Splash, Wobble, and Buddy designs each suit different use cases (freezing, suction-cup wall mounting, cats vs. dogs), which gives you legitimate cross-selling opportunities within the same brand.

WOOF is a newer enrichment-first brand gaining strong shelf velocity with pet specialty retailers. Their starter pack bundles multiple enrichment formats and is performing well as a gift-ready product for new pet owners.

Pricing Enrichment for Real Margin

This is where independent stores consistently underperform: pricing enrichment products too close to Amazon. Don't.

The typical price range for enrichment toys is $10–$45, with premium puzzle feeders reaching $50+. Customers shopping for enrichment are already in a "my pet's wellbeing is worth it" mindset — they are not comparison shopping the way they would for a commodity bag of kibble.

Target 45–60% gross margins on silicone lick mats and snuffle mats. Puzzle feeders from Nina Ottosson carry 40–50% margins at retail. Keystone pricing (100% markup over cost) is achievable on many enrichment SKUs, especially when you're the one providing the expertise and context that Amazon never does.

Bundle to lift average order value: a "new puppy enrichment starter kit" combining a KONG Classic, a LickiMat Splash, and a Nina Ottosson Level 1 puzzle at a 10% bundle discount creates a $65–$75 purchase that's irresistible to a new pet owner and generates strong margin for you.

Merchandising Enrichment Online: How to Do It Right

The biggest merchandising mistake stores make with enrichment: burying it in a generic "Toys" category. Enrichment deserves its own navigation destination.

Create a standalone "Enrichment & Mental Stimulation" collection page. Open with copy that explains why enrichment matters — 2 or 3 sentences on boredom, destructive behavior, and the mental workout analogy. Then merchandise by format (lick mats, puzzle feeders, snuffle mats) with brief explanations of each. Customers who land on a well-organized enrichment page convert at higher rates than those who arrive at a generic toy listing.

Product pages need "how to use" content. A lick mat page that includes 5 topping ideas, instructions for freezing (tip: freeze lick mats for 2–3x longer engagement), and a care/cleaning guide converts significantly better than a bare-bones listing. This is the expertise advantage you have over Chewy and Amazon — use it.

Add a difficulty guide for puzzle feeders. New pet owners are often intimidated by the Nina Ottosson line — they don't know where to start. A simple "Level 1 is perfect for beginners / Level 2 once your dog masters Level 1 / Level 3–4 for high-drive working breeds" removes the friction and enables the upsell journey over time.

Social Media Sells Enrichment Better Than Almost Anything

Pet content already generates 2.08x more engagement than average across social platforms. Enrichment product content performs even higher than that, because it's naturally emotional, funny, and satisfying to watch.

Your content playbook for enrichment:

  • The first try: Film a dog or cat encountering a new puzzle feeder for the first time. The confusion, the problem-solving, the eventual breakthrough — this format is consistently viral.
  • Lick mat topping recipes: A 30-second video showing how to layer peanut butter, banana, and kibble on a LickiMat gets saved and shared endlessly. Each recipe video functions as a soft sell for the mat itself.
  • Before and after: "This dog ate through 3 couches before we found puzzle feeders" is a narrative that resonates with millions of frustrated pet owners. It also sells product with zero hard pitch.
  • Difficulty challenges: Show the same dog progressing from Level 1 to Level 4 Nina Ottosson puzzles over a few weeks. This content series keeps customers engaged and pre-sells the upgrade cycle.

Dog enrichment content consistently sees 15–35% save rates on Instagram — meaning followers bookmark it for reference. Saved content is intent-signaling behavior. Run retargeting ads to those savers and your conversion rate will be materially higher than cold traffic.

Build Enrichment Into Your Email Flows

Enrichment products belong in several of your automated email sequences, not just promotional blasts:

New customer onboarding: After someone's first purchase (especially food or treats), send an email in week 2 titled "Are you enriching your pet's day?" with 3 product recommendations and the behavioral science context. This educates and cross-sells simultaneously.

Post-purchase for toy buyers: A customer who just bought a KONG Classic should get an email two weeks later about lick mat toppers and puzzle feeders. They're already in the enrichment mindset — help them build out the category.

Seasonal triggers: Summer heat keeps dogs indoors more. Winter keeps cats sedentary. Both are natural moments to push enrichment with messaging that connects the season to the behavioral need. "When it's too hot to walk, enrich instead" practically writes itself.

Replenishment reminders: Lick mats wear out. Puzzle feeders get lost or chewed. A replenishment or "try the next level" email 3–4 months after an enrichment purchase is a high-open, high-convert message that requires zero discount.

The Bottom Line

Enrichment isn't a trend. It's a category that's been hiding in plain sight — validated by veterinary behaviorists, supercharged by social media, and growing at a double-digit rate while most stores treat it as an accessory to their toy aisle.

The independent pet store that builds a genuine enrichment destination — with curated brands, expert content, tutorial videos, and bundle offers — wins this customer for life. Because the enrichment buyer is exactly the kind of pet parent you want: thoughtful, willing to spend on quality, motivated by their pet's wellbeing, and not price-shopping kibble on Amazon.

Start with four SKUs: a KONG Classic multi-pack, LickiMat Splash, Nina Ottosson Dog Brick Level 2, and a snuffle mat. Write three paragraphs of honest, helpful content for each. Post one enrichment video this week. You'll know within 30 days whether you've found your highest-engagement product category.
