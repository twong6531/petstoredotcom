Your Customers Are Upgrading Their Kibble Bowl. Here's How to Own the $7 Billion Topper Category Before Your Competitors Do.

📅 July 11, 2026 ✍️ PetStore.com Editorial Team 🏷️ Nutrition · Hybrid Feeding · Revenue

Your customers are not abandoning kibble. They're augmenting it — and they're spending more money doing it. The dog food topper market is worth $7 billion in 2026 and growing at a 13.2% CAGR through 2030. Every bag of kibble that leaves your store without a topper recommendation is a missed $12–$25 add-on sale. Industry analysts now call "hybrid feeding" — kibble plus freeze-dried crumbles, broth, or functional toppers — the single biggest shift in pet nutrition this decade. If your store doesn't have a topper strategy, you're watching an entire category walk out the door.

Why Hybrid Feeding Is the Biggest Shift in Pet Nutrition Right Now

For years, pet owners faced a binary choice: feed your dog kibble and feel a little guilty, or go full raw and deal with the expense, freezer space, and prep time. Hybrid feeding dissolves that choice entirely. Pet owners can keep their existing food routine and layer on fresh, raw, or functional nutrition in a way that feels premium but takes about ten seconds.

The trend is accelerating fast. Multiple 2026 reports confirm that hybrid formats — kibble plus freeze-dried, broths, or functional toppers — now dominate the innovation pipeline at pet food companies. At Global Pet Expo 2026, toppers and mixers represented one of the largest growth areas in the new products showcase. The market is evolving from an occasional treat category into a regular, daily nutrition solution. That means recurring purchase behavior — which means exactly the kind of repeat customer you want.

The driver is straightforward: 56% of dog owners and 54% of cat owners now actively seek digestive health, protein-quality, and functional nutrition claims on pet food. Toppers let them make a targeted upgrade without overhauling their entire feeding routine. That's a customer who needs a knowledgeable store to guide them — not an algorithm.

The Four Topper Formats Your Store Should Stock

Not all toppers are the same, and stocking the right mix of formats matters for both margin and customer education. Here's what the category actually looks like:

  • Freeze-dried crumbles and meal mixers — the category leader, with 82.5% dry-format market share. Shelf stable, high protein, and loaded with raw-food credibility. Products like Instinct Raw Boost Mixers and Stella & Chewy's Meal Mixers are the entry point for most new topper customers. Margins: 40–55%.
  • Bone broth and liquid toppers — the fastest-growing format, driven by convenience and palatability. Single-serve pouches with no prep required. The Honest Kitchen's broths and Open Farm's bone broth pouches sell well at $4–$7 per serving to owners who want maximum convenience. Cats are especially receptive to moisture-rich formats — this is your best cat cross-sell.
  • Sauce pouches and gravy toppers — single-serve, portion-controlled, and increasingly mainstream. Sauce formats are "decoupling topper purchases from bulk kibble replenishment cycles," per market analysts — meaning customers stock up on toppers independently of their food order. That's its own autoship line.
  • Functional powders — the newest entrant. Think collagen powders, probiotic blends, or superfood greens formulated to be mixed directly into any meal. These carry the highest functional claims and the highest price points. JAC Pet Nutrition's crumbles and brands like Vetri-Science Canine Plus lead here. Customers who buy functional powders are the most engaged segment of your topper audience.

The Best Brands for Independent Retailers

This category rewards curation over breadth. Stock 8–12 SKUs done right, not 40 options that overwhelm your customers. Here are the brands that earn shelf space:

Stella & Chewy's is the anchor brand for freeze-dried toppers — their Meal Mixers line has strong consumer recognition, solid margins (around 42–48% for indie accounts), and an ethos that aligns with premium raw nutrition. They belong at eye level in any topper display.

The Honest Kitchen makes the strongest broth and liquid toppers on the market. Human-grade certification is a genuine differentiator, and their 52-gram broth pouches at $4.99 each are a reliable impulse purchase at checkout.

Instinct by Nature's Variety — specifically their Freeze Dried Raw Boost Mixers — consistently win "best overall" rankings from independent reviewers and dog food advisors. Strong brand credibility for skeptical customers.

Primal Pet Foods and Smallbatch are the indie darlings — premium positioning, whole-food ingredients, and the kind of origin story customers love. Both offer wholesale programs worth pursuing.

Scout & Zoe's is worth a call if you want a wholesale-friendly partner with human-grade, USA-sourced toppers and no complicated minimum commitments — built specifically for smaller retailers.

Merchandising Toppers to Add $20+ to Every Kibble Sale

The worst place to merchandise toppers is in a separate "supplements" or "treats" aisle. The best place is physically next to your kibble section — specifically, adjacent to whichever kibble bags your customers buy most.

The merchandising principle is simple: remove the mental step. If a customer picking up a bag of Acana has to walk across the store to find a compatible topper, they won't. If there's a curated "upgrade this bag" display right next to the Acana shelf with three topper options and a short "why to add it" card, the attach rate jumps dramatically.

Online stores: create a "frequently bought together" module on every kibble product page featuring two or three toppers. Even a 15% attach rate on kibble orders — if your average kibble sale is $55 — adds $8–$12 per transaction to your revenue. At 200 orders per month, that's $1,600–$2,400 in incremental monthly revenue from one UX change.

Build bundles. A "Starter Hybrid Kit" — one bag of freeze-dried crumbles, one bone broth 4-pack — priced at a 10% discount to individual components is a strong conversion tool, especially for customers curious about raw nutrition but not ready to commit to a full raw diet.

The Functional Angle That Separates You From PetSmart

PetSmart carries Purina's version of a topper. You can carry something better, and more importantly, you can explain why it's better — which PetSmart cannot do.

The functional nutrition angle is where this category gets interesting for indie stores. Toppers now regularly incorporate glucosamine and chondroitin for joint support, specific probiotic strains (Lactobacillus acidophilus, Bifidobacterium longum), digestive enzymes, and anti-inflammatory adaptogens like turmeric and reishi. Brands like Primal and Smallbatch have toppers that function more like daily supplements than meal additions.

For stores that have built authority around nutrition, this opens a consulting conversation: "Your senior Lab is having hip trouble — I'd put him on a glucosamine-fortified topper twice a day before we think about prescription joint supplements." That's a recommendation a big-box store cannot make and an algorithm cannot replicate. And it converts at a dramatically higher rate than product pages alone.

Pricing, Margins, and What to Actually Expect

The topper category consistently delivers 40–65% gross margins for indie pet stores — significantly higher than the 20–30% typical on kibble. Here's how the math typically works:

  • Freeze-dried crumbles (5.5 oz bag) — retail $14.99–$18.99, wholesale $7–$9, margin 45–53%
  • Bone broth pouches (4-pack) — retail $19.99–$24.99, wholesale $9–$12, margin 45–55%
  • Functional powder (60-day supply) — retail $34.99–$49.99, wholesale $14–$22, margin 45–60%

Topper customers also reorder faster than almost any other category. A 5.5-oz bag of crumbles used daily disappears in 15–30 days depending on dog size. A 4-pack of broth pouches is gone in a week for enthusiastic users. Reorder velocity in this category rivals treats and is substantially faster than most supplements.

Why Toppers Are Your Perfect Autoship Category

Here's the structural advantage toppers have over almost everything else in your store: they are daily, consumable, and variable. Pet owners rotate proteins — beef this month, salmon next month, chicken after that. That rotation creates a natural autoship habit where customers subscribe to a rotation, not a single SKU.

Modular, single-serve packaging is already training customers to think of toppers as a distinct purchase from their main food order. They stock up on broth separately from kibble. They run out of crumbles mid-month and reorder without waiting for their next food shipment. That purchase decoupling is gold for your autoship metrics.

An autoship on a $16.99 topper bag at 15% off — roughly $14.45 per month — is small enough that customers don't cancel it during budget reviews. It has an extremely high retention rate because it's a daily ritual, not an occasional purchase. And it keeps your store top-of-mind every single month even during periods when the customer isn't buying food.

If you have 500 autoship customers and 30% of them add a topper subscription, that's 150 additional monthly autoship subscribers — generating roughly $2,170 in recurring monthly revenue at virtually zero acquisition cost.

The Bottom Line

The food topper revolution isn't a niche trend — it's a $7 billion category growing at 13.2% annually, driven by exactly the kind of health-conscious, premium-focused pet owner who shops at independent stores. The big-box players stock it without understanding it. You can stock it, explain it, and build an entire recurring revenue engine around it.

Start with four to six SKUs across two or three formats, merchandise them adjacent to your top-selling kibble, and pitch the bundle at checkout. You don't need to overhaul your store — you need to add a display and a conversation.
