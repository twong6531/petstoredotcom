Your Emails Get 20% Opens. Your Texts Get 98%. The SMS Marketing Playbook Every Pet Store Needs.

📅 July 4, 2026 ✍️ PetStore.com Editorial Team 🏷️ Marketing · SMS · Ecommerce

Here's a stat that should make you put down your coffee: the average marketing email gets a 20% open rate. The average SMS gets 98% — and 90% of those texts are read within three minutes of delivery. While you've been optimizing subject lines and A/B testing preview text, your customers have been carrying a channel in their pocket that converts at nearly 5× the rate. Most independent pet stores aren't using it yet. That's your window.

Why SMS Hits Different for Pet Stores Specifically

SMS marketing works across every ecommerce vertical, but pet stores have structural advantages that make it especially potent. Think about what you sell: consumables. Pet food runs out. Flea treatments have a calendar. Supplements get reordered every 30–60 days like clockwork.

Those repeat-purchase cycles are SMS goldmines. A well-timed text — "Hey, Tucker's food is probably running low — here's your reorder link" — lands when the customer is already thinking about the problem. That's not spam. That's service.

Add the emotional dimension: pet owners are among the most engaged, passionate buyers in consumer goods. A birthday text for their dog with a discount code doesn't feel like marketing. It feels like you know them. That's the kind of relationship Chewy's algorithm cannot fake.

The Numbers That Should Convince Your Skeptical Side

Before we get into tactics, here's why this channel earns its budget:

  • Average SMS marketing ROI: $71 for every $1 spent (email's ROI tops out around $36)
  • Abandoned-cart SMS conversion rate: 24.6%–39.4%
  • Cart recovery texts generate up to $5.60 per message sent
  • Automated SMS flows represent just 7.6% of sends but drive 45.2% of total SMS revenue
  • One mid-sized pet-care ecommerce brand that segmented its SMS list by pet type saw a 20% higher click-through rate and 15% AOV lift compared to generic blasts

The channel isn't a gimmick. For DTC brands that do it properly, SMS now accounts for 10–20% of total ecommerce revenue.

The Five Automated Flows That Do 80% of the Work

Don't start with broadcast campaigns. Start with flows — automated messages triggered by customer behavior. This is where the real money is. A single well-configured flow runs forever without touching it again.

1. Abandoned cart (send within 30 minutes). This is your highest-ROI flow. Someone added the grain-free salmon kibble to their cart and got distracted. A text that says "You left something behind — and Tucker looks hungry 🐶" with a direct checkout link converts at 24–39%. Set a second message for 24 hours later if no action was taken. That's it. Two texts, recovers hundreds in monthly revenue.

2. Post-purchase upsell (send 2–3 days after delivery). Someone just bought a new harness. They're in a good mood — the product arrived, their dog loves it. Text them about the matching leash or a training treat sampler. This is the warm hand-off moment email marketers have been trying to replicate for years.

3. Replenishment reminder (triggered by average consume rate). If a customer bought a 15-lb bag of food, they're probably out in 30–45 days. Build a flow that triggers at day 28 with a reorder link and a gentle "don't let Bella run out" message. Add a 5% discount for autoship signup and this flow effectively becomes a subscriber acquisition engine.

4. Pet birthday or adoption anniversary (automated annually). Collect pet names and birthdays in your signup flow, loyalty program, or post-purchase survey. A birthday text with a 10% discount code feels personal in a way a promotional blast never does. Open rates on these exceed 95%.

5. Win-back (triggered at 90 days since last purchase). "We miss [pet name]! Here's 15% off their favorites" is consistently one of the highest-converting messages any pet store sends. It's non-invasive, emotionally intelligent, and arrives at exactly the moment a lapsed customer might be ready to come back.

How to Build Your SMS List From Zero

A powerful flow setup means nothing without an engaged list. Here's what actually moves the subscriber count:

Checkout opt-in. The highest-converting moment is at checkout — customers are already in buying mode and trust you with their payment info. A single checkbox ("Text me order updates and exclusive deals") typically converts 30–40% of buyers into SMS subscribers. This is non-negotiable.

Pop-up with a real offer. A generic "join our SMS list" popup converts at 2–3%. A pop-up offering "Text JOIN to get 15% off your first order" converts at 8–12%. The difference is giving subscribers something in exchange for their number. Tools like Klaviyo, Postscript, and Attentive all have pop-up builders with mobile-optimized templates.

Social promotion. Add a link-in-bio CTA on Instagram or TikTok pointing to your SMS signup page. A short video — "Sign up for texts and we'll send you Tucker's Weekly Deal" — works surprisingly well with pet-loving audiences who already engage with your content.

In-package insert. If you ship orders, include a card that says "Text PAWS to [your number] to get 10% off your next order." Physical to digital conversion rates are low, but the subscribers you get tend to be highly engaged.

Platform Showdown: Klaviyo vs. Postscript vs. Attentive

You have three serious options, each with a different philosophy:

Klaviyo is the right choice if you're already using it for email (which you should be). The unified customer profiles — seeing SMS clicks, email opens, and purchase history in one place — enables segmentation that standalone tools cannot match. Pricing starts at around $0.01–$0.02 per SMS sent, which gets expensive at scale but is unbeatable for stores under $500K in revenue. Native Shopify integration is seamless.

Postscript is built SMS-first for Shopify merchants and shines at conversational texting — two-way messaging where customers can actually text back questions. If your store prioritizes customer service and you want to respond to inbound texts, Postscript's interface handles that better than Klaviyo. Plans start at $100/month for smaller lists.

Attentive is the enterprise tier — best for stores doing $2M+ annually who want a dedicated account team and advanced conversational AI features. It's more expensive ($500+/month), but brands on the platform see some of the industry's highest revenue-per-message numbers because of sophisticated segmentation and A/B testing capabilities.

For most independent pet stores: start with Klaviyo if you're already on it, or Postscript if SMS is going to be your primary growth channel.

Segmentation Is What Separates $0.71 from $5.60 Per Message

The average ecommerce SMS campaign generates $0.71 per message sent. Cart abandonment recovery texts generate up to $5.60 per message. The difference isn't magic — it's relevance.

The fastest segmentation wins for pet stores:

  • By pet type — dog owners get dog content; cat owners get cat content. Never send a "perfect for your pup" message to a cat household.
  • By purchase history — raw food buyers want raw food news and restocking nudges, not deals on kibble
  • By engagement tier — send promotional blasts only to subscribers who've clicked in the last 90 days; exclude cold subscribers to protect your delivery rates
  • By autoship status — existing autoship members should get loyalty perks and cross-sell offers, not "subscribe and save" recruitment pitches they're already enrolled in

Klaviyo's conditional splits make this easy to automate. You set the rules once; every new subscriber routes correctly from there.

TCPA Compliance: The Rules That Protect You and Your Customers

SMS marketing is regulated by the Telephone Consumer Protection Act, and the FCC has tightened consent rules in recent years. The key requirements are not complicated, but the penalties for violations are steep ($500–$1,500 per violation).

The non-negotiables: express written consent before any marketing text (a checkbox at checkout or a keyword opt-in counts), a clear opt-out mechanism in every message ("Reply STOP to unsubscribe"), identification of who's texting in every message, and messaging only between 8am–9pm in the recipient's local time zone.

Every major platform — Klaviyo, Postscript, Attentive — handles TCPA-compliant opt-in flows out of the box. Don't build consent management yourself. Use their tools, keep your opt-in records, and you're covered.

The Bottom Line

SMS marketing isn't a shiny new toy — it's a mature, data-proven revenue channel that most independent pet stores still haven't touched. While your competitors are fighting over email subject-line optimization, you could be building a list where 98% of your messages actually get seen.

Start with one thing: the checkout opt-in. Get that live this week. Then set up the abandoned-cart flow. Those two moves alone will generate positive ROI within 30 days. Everything else on this list — segmentation, birthday flows, platform migration — comes after the fundamentals are working.

Your customers are already on their phones. Meet them there.
