36.8% of Pet Searches Now Trigger AI Answers — Here's How to Get Your Store Inside Them

📅 June 15, 2026 ✍️ PetStore.com Editorial Team 🏷️ SEO · AI Search · Ecommerce

Here's a number that should make every pet store owner stop scrolling: zero-click Google searches have jumped from 56% to 69% of all searches in the last 18 months. That means more than two-thirds of people who search on Google never click a single result. They get their answer directly from an AI-generated box at the top of the page and move on. For pet-specific queries, it's even more concentrated — 36.8% of pet-related searches now trigger a Google AI Overview, and "best grain-free dog food for sensitive stomachs" carries an 83% AI Overview presence. If your pet store isn't being cited inside those AI answers, you're invisible to millions of buyers who never scroll past the box. Here's how to fix that.

The Zero-Click Earthquake Has Already Hit Your Analytics

If your organic traffic has dropped 20–40% over the past year on informational queries — "best raw food for puppies," "are fish oil supplements safe for cats," "how often should I feed a senior dog" — you're not doing anything wrong. You're experiencing the structural shift to AI-mediated search.

Gartner projected that commercial website organic traffic would decline 25% by 2026 as consumers shift discovery to AI platforms. That projection was conservative. The shift is real, it's here, and it's accelerating. The stores that are growing organic traffic right now have all made the same pivot: they stopped optimizing to rank on page one and started optimizing to be cited inside AI answers. That's a different game with different rules.

The good news: independent pet stores are structurally better positioned to win this game than Chewy or Amazon, because the content that wins AI citations — specific, expert, deeply helpful — is exactly the content small operators can create authentically.

Why Ranking #1 No Longer Guarantees Clicks (And What Does)

For years, the SEO goal was simple: get to position one on Google, collect traffic. That model is broken for most pet-related informational queries. When someone searches "best dog food for joint pain," Google's AI Overview synthesizes an answer from multiple sources — and the pages it cites get a fraction of the clicks they used to. Ranking #3 and being cited in the AI Overview now beats ranking #1 and being excluded from it.

What gets you cited? Three things matter most, according to analysis of which pages appear in AI-generated answers:

  • Authoritative, specific content — not "dog food is important for health" but "for large breeds over 50 lbs with hip dysplasia, glucosamine-fortified kibble combined with a fish oil topper at 1,000mg EPA/DHA per day is the most evidence-backed nutritional approach"
  • Structured data markup — pages with comprehensive schema are cited 3–5x more frequently in AI shopping recommendations
  • Strong review signals — AI systems treat review volume and recency as a proxy for trustworthiness

Meanwhile, pure transactional queries like "buy dog food online" or specific branded product names still carry only 13–14% AI Overview presence. That means your product pages are largely safe — it's your category and educational content where the ground has shifted.

Schema Markup: The Technical Fix That Gets You Cited 3–5x More Often

This is the highest-leverage technical change most pet stores aren't making. An analysis of 180 ecommerce sites found that pages with comprehensive schema markup receive 2.7x more impressions and appear 3–5x more often in AI-generated shopping recommendations than pages without it.

The critical distinction: generic Product schema doesn't move the needle. What the AI citation engines respond to is attribute-rich schema — concrete pricing, ratings, specifications, availability, and return policy details all included.

For a Shopify store, start here this week:

  • Install the Schema Plus for SEO app ($14/month) — it handles Product, Review, AggregateRating, and BreadcrumbList schema automatically
  • Audit your Google Merchant Center feed: every product needs a full attribute set — price, brand, condition, availability, GTIN or MPN, and high-resolution images. Products with 95%+ attribute completion get priority placement in Google AI Shopping
  • Add FAQPage schema to your buying guide and breed-specific content pages — these are the pages most likely to be cited in informational AI Overviews
  • Check your robots.txt to confirm you're allowing OAI-SearchBot (OpenAI's crawler) — blocking it effectively makes your site invisible to ChatGPT Shopping results

Note: Google deprecated FAQ schema for standard product pages in January 2026, but it remains valid and effective on editorial and guide pages. Don't strip it from your blog content.

How to Get Recommended by ChatGPT Shopping

ChatGPT Shopping launched in earnest in 2025 and has become a meaningful traffic source for pet ecommerce stores. Here's how it works differently from Google: ChatGPT Shopping is better for early-funnel discovery — someone who types "what should I feed my dog with kidney disease" into ChatGPT is asking a question, not ready to buy yet. But ChatGPT Shopping links them to products as part of the conversational answer, and those visitors, when they arrive, convert up to 23x higher than traditional organic search traffic because they've already been pre-qualified by the AI conversation.

To get your products into ChatGPT Shopping results, you need three things:

  • Allow OAI-SearchBot in your robots.txt (most Shopify themes block it by default — check yours now)
  • Complete JSON-LD Product schema with server-side rendering — client-side rendered schema is frequently missed by AI crawlers
  • Buyer-guide content on your site that answers the exact conversational questions your customers ask — "what protein source is best for dogs with pancreatitis," "how do I transition my cat to a raw diet," etc. ChatGPT cites pages that answer questions, then surfaces products from those same domains

The brands winning ChatGPT Shopping aren't the biggest — they're the ones with the most thorough, genuinely useful educational content linked to their product catalog.

Problem-First Buying Guides: The Content Format That Wins AI Overviews

Here's the content strategy shift that matters most. Stop writing product-first content ("Introducing our new freeze-dried salmon topper"). Start writing problem-first content that answers the exact query someone types into a search bar or AI assistant.

The highest-AI-Overview-capture content format for pet stores is the condition-specific buying guide: "The Best Dog Foods for Senior Dogs with Kidney Disease (Vet-Reviewed, 2026)" or "5 Supplements That Actually Help Cats with Arthritis." These pages rank for long-tail searches and get cited in AI Overviews because they're exactly what the AI is trying to synthesize for the user.

Specific format tips that increase AI citation rates:

  • Include a clear verdict or recommendation near the top — AI systems extract the answer, not just the context
  • Use specific numbers and named products: "We recommend Orijen Senior at $89/15 lbs for its 38% protein content from 85% animal ingredients"
  • Add a "Last updated" date — freshness is a signal AI crawlers use to prioritize content
  • Structure with H2 and H3 headers that mirror common question phrasings — "Is grain-free food safe for dogs?" not just "Grain-Free Food"

A store that publishes 30 well-researched, problem-first guides owns an asset library that compounds in both traditional rankings and AI citations for years at zero marginal cost per click.

Reviews Are Now AI Ranking Signals — Here's How to Stack Them

AI search systems — both Google's and ChatGPT's — treat review volume and recency as trust signals. A pet store with 400 recent, detailed reviews will consistently outrank a technically superior site with 40 thin reviews. And the gap is not subtle: AI-driven search results "strongly favor brands that have strong reviews and consistent online activity," according to 2026 analysis from Petbase.

The fastest way to build review velocity on a Shopify store:

  • Okendo ($19/month) automates post-purchase review requests with photo/video prompts and syncs directly to Google Shopping and your product schema — photo reviews improve AI citation rate further
  • Set up a review request sequence in Klaviyo: Day 7 post-delivery for food and supplements (enough time to see results), Day 14 for accessories
  • Reply to every review — publicly. Google's systems use response engagement as a freshness signal, and your responses become indexed content
  • Import existing reviews from Facebook, Etsy, or any previous platform before you start building new ones — every review is an AI-readable data point

A steady cadence of 15–20 new reviews per month is enough to maintain freshness signals that keep your content competitive in AI citation ranking.

Your 5-Step AI Search Quick-Start for Pet Stores

If you do nothing else from this article, do these five things in order:

  1. Check robots.txt today. Make sure OAI-SearchBot and Googlebot are allowed. This is a 10-minute fix that can open up ChatGPT Shopping visibility immediately.
  2. Install Schema Plus for SEO on Shopify and run a Google Rich Results Test on your top 10 product pages. Fix any errors before anything else.
  3. Audit your Merchant Center feed. Every product should have brand, GTIN/MPN, description over 150 characters, and at least one high-resolution image. Fill gaps product by product, starting with your bestsellers.
  4. Write one problem-first buying guide this week. Pick your best-selling product category and write the definitive 1,200-word guide answering the top 5 questions customers ask before buying. Update the date monthly.
  5. Set up automated review requests in Klaviyo. A 7-day post-delivery sequence asking for a review with a photo prompt will build your citation-grade review library faster than any other single action.

None of these requires a developer or an agency. All five can be done by a solo operator in a weekend. The stores doing them right now are building a compounding advantage over competitors who are still chasing page one rankings on queries that no longer deliver the traffic they used to.
