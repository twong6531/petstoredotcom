Smart Feeders, GPS Trackers, and Connected Cameras: The High-Margin Pet Tech Category Most Stores Are Sleeping On

📅 June 12, 2026 ✍️ PetStore.com Editorial Team 🏷️ Pet Tech · Ecommerce · Strategy

Here's a number that should bother you: the global smart pet tech market is valued at $19.1 billion in 2026 and is on track to hit $52.9 billion by 2035. That's a 12% compound annual growth rate — faster than raw food, faster than supplements, faster than almost any other category in the entire pet industry.

Now ask yourself: how many smart pet tech products are on your website right now?

If the answer is "zero" or "a couple of things I added almost by accident," you're not alone — but you're leaving significant money on the table. Most independent online pet stores are still organized around food, treats, and accessories. Those categories are fine. But smart pet tech is the fastest-growing subcategory in the industry, it attracts a different (higher-spending) buyer, and the margins are dramatically better than dog food. Here's what that opportunity looks like up close — and exactly how to go after it.

Why Pet Tech Is Hitting Its Stride Right Now

Smart pet products — connected feeders, GPS trackers, health-monitoring wearables, automated litter boxes, in-home cameras — have been around for a few years. What's different in 2026 is that they've crossed the chasm from early adopter to mainstream. A few forces are converging at once.

Pet humanization is accelerating. The 2026 Elanco research surveyed thousands of pet owners and found that 95% consider pet health a protected expense they would not reduce, even during financial hardship. When your dog is genuinely a family member, a smart feeder that portions their meals correctly while you're traveling stops being a luxury gadget and becomes a necessity.

The highest-income buyers are entering the category. Gen X — which saw a 12% increase in pet ownership in 2025 and represents the highest-earning pet owner segment — is driving smart tech adoption. These are people with disposable income, comfort with technology, and real anxiety about their pets' wellbeing when they're at work or away. They're the ideal smart pet tech customer.

The hardware and software finally works. The connectivity issues that plagued early smart pet products in 2019–2022 have been largely solved. Products are more reliable, apps are genuinely good, and return rates have dropped. Buyers are recommending, not regretting.

The Five Categories to Stock First

Not all smart pet tech is equal. Here's where to start — ranked by market share and ease of selling online.

1. Smart Automatic Feeders (29% of market share)
The largest segment by far. App-controlled feeders let owners schedule meals, set exact portions, and often include a built-in camera for face-to-face feeding sessions. The Pawsync Smart Pet Feeder uses AI recognition to automatically detect which pet is at the bowl — a critical feature for multi-pet households where each animal needs different food. Unit cost: $25–40. Retail price: $79–149. Gross margins: 45–65%.

2. GPS Pet Trackers (18.3% CAGR — the fastest-growing segment)
Tractive GPS 4 and Whistle Switch are the U.S. category leaders. These LTE-connected trackers provide real-time location, activity monitoring, and sleep tracking from a smartphone app. For anxious pet parents — and that's essentially every pet parent — this product sells itself. Unit cost: $40–60. Retail: $79–149. Many include a monthly subscription component, which can generate recurring referral revenue if you structure it correctly with the manufacturer.

3. Pet Cameras with Two-Way Audio
Furbo and Petcube are the established leaders, but Eufy and Tapo have pushed quality up and prices down across the category. These cameras let owners check on, talk to, and treat their pets remotely. They're strong impulse purchases — especially for new puppy owners — and they have among the highest satisfaction scores in any pet product category. Easy to photograph, easy to demo on video, and easy to gift.

4. Smart Health Monitoring Wearables
FitBark Pro+ sold over 2.1 million units in Q1 2026 alone, outpacing all competitors by 37%. Whistle Health combines GPS and health monitoring in a single collar attachment. These products generate ongoing owner engagement through data dashboards, which makes buyers more health-conscious about their pets — and more likely to purchase your supplements, functional toppers, and wellness products. The cross-sell potential is enormous.

5. Self-Cleaning Smart Litter Boxes
Higher-ticket ($400–600 retail), but the margins are exceptional and the repeat purchase behavior is locked in. Once a cat owner switches to a Litter-Robot or Neakasa M1 Plus, they essentially never go back. These products also have consumable components — liners, deodorizing cartridges — that are natural recurring purchases. One smart litter box sale can create years of consumable revenue.

The Margin Math That Makes This Worth It

Here's the part that should get your attention if you've been on the fence.

Standard pet food margins hover around 20–35% depending on brand and volume. Premium supplements — which we've covered as a strong category — hit 40–60%. Smart pet tech sits at 45–65% gross margin on most categories, with significantly higher average order values ($89–149 per transaction) and virtually no shipping weight penalty.

A GPS tracker ships in a padded envelope. A smart feeder ships in a box the size of a coffee maker. Your margins on a 30-pound bag of kibble get crushed by dimensional weight shipping costs. Your margins on a $99 connected feeder don't.

The other math that matters: smart pet tech buyers skew higher-income, convert at higher rates, and complain less. They're more likely to write reviews, recommend your store to friends, and come back to buy the next device in the category. These are exactly the customers you want to acquire.

How to Sell Tech to Non-Techie Pet Owners

The biggest mistake independent stores make with pet tech is treating it like any other product — listing the spec sheet and hoping for the best. That strategy does not work here.

The effective approach is problem-first content. Lead with the anxiety, not the features:

  • "Worried about your dog home alone for nine hours? This $89 camera sends you a notification when they bark."
  • "Feeding two cats on completely different diets? This feeder recognizes their faces and portions each meal separately."
  • "Your cat went missing for three days last winter. Here's the $79 solution."

Product pages need to be 400–600 words, not 80. Short-form video showing the app interface in actual use dramatically increases conversion — this content performs particularly well in Instagram Reels and TikTok. And "honest review" posts that include real pros and cons build the trust that turns browsers into buyers faster than any ad spend.

Your advantage over Amazon is concrete: you can explain exactly why a specific product is right for a specific type of owner — a senior dog with arthritis, a multi-pet household, a cat with anxiety, a first-time puppy parent working full-time. A marketplace cannot do that. You can.

Bundle Pet Tech with Consumables for Maximum Impact

The highest-leverage move in this category is the tech-plus-consumable bundle, and it works in both directions.

A smart feeder bundled with a bag of your best-selling premium kibble and a trial-size digestive supplement is a compelling launch package — and it simultaneously introduces pet tech buyers to your food catalog. Conversely, someone who's been buying raw food from you for six months is an ideal upsell target for a Furbo camera or FitBark wearable. They already trust your curation.

Use your post-purchase email flows — Klaviyo if you're on Shopify — to introduce your pet tech range to existing food customers 30 days after their first order. The pitch writes itself: "You already feed them the best food. Now make sure they're safe and healthy when you're not home."

This approach also works beautifully as a retention play. Pet tech buyers who also buy consumables from you have dramatically higher lifetime value than single-category customers. They're harder to poach, because switching costs go up every time they add a product category to your store.

Start Small. Stock Right. Own the Category.

You don't need 50 pet tech SKUs to make this work. You need 5–8 products that are the honest best choice in each category, priced right, with excellent content backing them up.

A practical starting point: one smart feeder (Pawsync or PetSafe Smart Feed), one GPS tracker (Tractive GPS 4), one camera (Furbo 360 or Petcube Play), and one health monitor (FitBark Pro+). That's roughly $300–400 in initial inventory across four SKUs. Write honest, problem-focused product pages for each. Send one email to your existing customer list.

Then watch who buys. Those customers — their questions, their reviews, their repeat behavior — will tell you exactly what to stock next. The smart pet tech category rewards expertise and curation. Independent stores that get there early, stock thoughtfully, and build real content authority will own a growing share of a $52.9 billion market by 2035.

That window is open right now. Most of your competitors haven't walked through it yet.

The Bottom Line

Smart pet tech isn't a niche curiosity anymore — it's a mainstream, high-margin, fast-growing category with buyers who convert easily, spend more, and stick around. You don't need to become a tech store. You need to add 5–8 carefully chosen connected products, write content that leads with the pet owner's anxiety rather than a spec sheet, and let the margins and the cross-sell potential do the rest.
