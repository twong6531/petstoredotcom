The Farmer's Dog Won't Wholesale to You — Here's the Human-Grade Pet Food Playbook for Indie Stores

📅 July 7, 2026 ✍️ PetStore.com Editorial Team 🏷️ Nutrition · Premium Food · Merchandising

The Farmer's Dog — the company delivering USDA human-grade dog food in biodegradable packaging — is reportedly worth over $1.4 billion. You cannot stock it. It's direct-to-consumer only, and they have no intention of wholesaling. But the trend they're riding? That is completely open to you. The global human-grade pet food market hit $1.65 billion in 2024 and is projected to reach $6.66 billion by 2033, growing at a 16.8% CAGR. North America accounts for 65% of that market. And the brands that are available to independent retailers are some of the fastest-moving SKUs in the industry right now. Here's your playbook.

What "Human-Grade" Actually Means (and Why It's Not Just Marketing)

"Human-grade" is a legal term regulated by AAFCO — the Association of American Feed Control Officials — not a loose marketing claim. For a pet food to be labeled human-grade, every ingredient must be edible for humans and the product must be manufactured, packed, and held in a USDA-inspected human food facility. If those two conditions aren't both true, the label is illegal.

This is meaningfully different from "premium," "natural," or "real meat first" — all of which have either loose or no regulatory definition. Human-grade means the USDA turkey in the bag is the same turkey that might land in a grocery store. The manufacturing line was inspected to the same standard as a deli counter.

That distinction is starting to matter enormously to pet owners. A Nielsen survey found that 76% of pet parents actively seek products that can improve their pets' health and wellness. For the millennial and Gen Z buyer — who reads ingredient lists the way their parents read car specs — human-grade isn't a premium; it's a baseline expectation they're willing to pay for.

The Market Numbers You Need to See

Before we get into brands and tactics, let's establish why this category deserves a real slot on your site right now:

  • Global market: $1.65B in 2024 → $6.66B by 2033 at a 16.8% CAGR
  • North America specifically: $1.19B in 2024 → $3.60B by 2034, growing at 17.5% annually
  • 67% of U.S. dog owners believe their pets require tailored, high-quality diets
  • 65% of millennial pet owners say they'll pay a premium for customized or human-quality nutrition
  • Average annual spend per pet is projected to hit $1,445 in 2026

The Farmer's Dog, Nom Nom, and Ollie have collectively raised hundreds of millions of dollars betting on this category — and they've validated the demand. What they haven't done is make the category available through independent retail. That's your opening.

The Brands Worth Stocking in 2026

The D2C giants are off-limits for retail. But there's a strong bench of human-grade and human-grade-inspired brands available through wholesale distribution right now:

Freshpet is the most widely distributed refrigerated pet food brand in the country, available through specialty retailers and grocery chains. Their Vital and Homestyle Creation lines use USDA-sourced chicken, beef, and salmon cooked in human food-standard facilities. Freshpet (FRPT) is publicly traded and has been aggressively expanding retail distribution — they want independent pet store partners.

Just Food for Dogs is as close to Farmer's Dog quality as retail gets. JFFD is USDA-certified and makes their meals with whole-food, human-grade ingredients. Their Pantry Fresh shelf-stable line is retail-friendly for online stores, and select retailers can partner for fresh meal programs. Their margins run 40-50% at suggested retail.

Weruva — their BFF and Cats in the Kitchen lines are made in human food facilities in Thailand and the U.S., with restaurant-quality protein sourcing including line-caught fish, free-range chicken, and humanely raised beef. Excellent packaging, strong brand story, retail-ready.

Open Farm is Certified Humane and Non-GMO Project Verified with a full trace-back system — every customer can enter a batch code and see exactly which farm their pet's chicken came from. Their Gently Cooked and raw frozen lines are available through specialty distribution, and their marketing assets were built for the exact discerning buyer you want.

Instinct Raw offers freeze-dried raw, raw frozen, and raw-boosted kibble combinations made with proteins they source to human-grade quality standards. A great entry point for customers curious about raw who aren't ready to commit fully.

The Refrigerated vs. Shelf-Stable Decision

Fresh refrigerated pet food carries the most powerful premium story — but it requires refrigeration infrastructure. For an online store, shipping fresh food means insulated packaging, dry ice or gel packs, and careful logistics coordination. Spoilage, temperature damage, and returns become real operational variables.

The smarter starting point for most online-first stores: lead with shelf-stable human-grade options and add refrigerated as a premium tier later.

Open Farm's Gently Cooked (frozen, but ships safely with overnight or 2-day plus insulated packaging), Weruva's pouch and can formats, Instinct's freeze-dried lines, and Just Food for Dogs' Pantry Fresh all deliver the human-grade story without the refrigeration headache. Once your human-grade revenue justifies the investment, add a Freshpet or fresh meal line for your highest-LTV customers with white-glove shipping.

Price benchmarks to anchor your product range:

  • Premium kibble: $2–$4/lb
  • Freeze-dried raw: $8–$15/lb
  • Shelf-stable human-grade: $6–$12/lb
  • Fresh refrigerated human-grade: $8–$16/lb (plus a shipping premium of $12–$20)

Pricing Strategy: When Your Margin Is Also Your Message

Human-grade pet food costs 2-5x more than conventional premium food, and selling it requires a different approach than "it's expensive but worth it." Three framing strategies that actually convert:

Cost per day, not cost per bag. A 4-lb bag of Open Farm Gently Cooked at $38 sounds steep. But at the recommended portion for a 25-lb dog, that's $3.60/day — less than a single Starbucks drink. Reframe every product page to show daily cost prominently. Most premium brands publish the math; borrow it.

The vet bill comparison. Diet-related health issues — obesity, dental disease, kidney problems, chronic skin conditions — cost pet owners thousands of dollars annually. Human-grade, whole-food diets are associated with improved outcomes across all four categories. Position premium food as preventive healthcare investment, not indulgence. "Spend $3.60/day now or $350/month at the vet" is a real calculation modern pet owners are making.

The ingredient contrast. Run a side-by-side comparison between a leading conventional kibble and your best human-grade line directly on the product page. "Chicken meal vs. USDA whole chicken. Corn syrup vs. organic sweet potato. Artificial coloring vs. none." The contrast closes itself for any pet owner who's ever read a label.

Merchandising Human-Grade on Your Website

A $36 bag of human-grade food does not sell itself the way a $12 bag of kibble does. The content investment scales with the price point. Here's what actually moves this category online:

Put a 60-90 second "How It's Made" video on every brand page. Freshpet, Open Farm, and Just Food for Dogs all provide brand-produced video assets you can embed. A customer watching actual whole chicken being portioned in a kitchen-grade facility is sold by the time the video ends.

Add a daily cost calculator widget to human-grade product pages. Enter your pet's weight and breed, get a daily cost and a suggested bag size. Several Shopify apps (like Infinite Options or a custom metafield) can power this with minimal development work.

Every human-grade product page needs a 10-day transition guide. Switching a dog's food abruptly causes GI upset — which means returns, refunds, and a bad experience. A built-in "how to switch" section at the bottom of every page eliminates 80% of those issues and signals real expertise to first-time buyers.

Create a dedicated "Human-Grade & Fresh Food" category page in your navigation. Don't bury these products in a generic Nutrition tab. The customer searching for this category is motivated and ready to spend — make it easy to find.

Finally, bundle with digestive supplements at the product level. The customer buying Open Farm Gently Cooked is the exact same customer who'll buy probiotics, omega-3s, and a digestive enzyme. Build a "Complete the Bowl" add-on directly on the product page as a one-click bundle.

Converting Human-Grade Buyers Into Autoship Subscribers

Here's the part that makes human-grade a category worth building around: once a pet accepts a new food and the owner sees improved coat quality, energy levels, and digestive health — they don't go back. The churn rate from premium human-grade food back to conventional kibble is remarkably low. This buyer is your highest-LTV customer segment, period.

Structure your autoship pitch to capture them on the first order. Show the 15% subscription discount prominently on every human-grade product page — not buried in fine print, but directly next to the one-time price. Make the comparison impossible to miss.

At the 60-day mark, trigger an automated email: "How is [pet name] doing on their new food?" with a re-order button embedded. This type of check-in email pulls 35-45% open rates from premium food buyers — they care about their pet's response to the food. You're not sending marketing; you're doing customer service. Attach a product recommendation for a complementary supplement and you've just added to average order value with zero friction.

Give human-grade buyers an elevated loyalty tier from purchase one. They're spending 2-5x more than your average customer — recognize it. Early access to new arrivals, free sample packs with orders, and handwritten notes on the first shipment all cost almost nothing and create the kind of emotional connection that makes customers feel like they joined something, not just bought something.

The Farmer's Dog built a $1.4 billion business by making pet owners feel like they were doing right by their animal. You can create the same feeling — with the added advantage of carrying multiple top-quality brands, offering real human expertise, and building a community that their algorithm-driven subscription service never can.

Where to Start This Week

If you're not carrying any human-grade food today, start with Open Farm and Weruva — both have strong distributor networks, excellent brand assets, and shelf-stable lines that work perfectly for online shipping. Create your dedicated category page, add a daily cost framing to every product description, and set up your first human-grade autoship offer.

You don't need to stock every brand or build a refrigeration program on day one. You need to claim the category before your local competitor does — because once a customer finds their pet's new favorite food at a store they trust, the repeat purchase is automatic.
