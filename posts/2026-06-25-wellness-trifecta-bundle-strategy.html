The Wellness Trifecta: Why Bundling Supplements, Dental Care, and Functional Treats Is Your Highest-Leverage Move Right Now

📅 June 25, 2026 ✍️ PetStore.com Editorial Team 🏷️ Merchandising · AOV · Wellness

Here's a stat that should change how you merchandise your store today: 56% of dog owners and 54% of cat owners are now actively seeking "digestive health" claims when they shop, and the customers buying supplements are the same ones buying dental care and functional treats — usually within the same order, if you let them. Most independent pet stores still sell these three categories as if they have nothing to do with each other. That's money walking out the door.

What the "Wellness Trifecta" Actually Means

Industry data is now tracking a distinct shopper segment: pet parents who regularly buy supplements, dental products, and functional treats together as one ongoing wellness routine, not three separate purchases. This isn't a niche — it's becoming the default behavior of the highest-spending pet owner in your store.

It makes sense once you think about it. A customer who buys a hip-and-joint chew for their senior dog is exactly the kind of owner who also wants dental chews and a gut-health topper. They're not browsing categories. They're building a daily health stack for their pet. If your store presents these as three unrelated aisles, you're making that customer do the merchandising work themselves — and a lot of them won't bother.

The Numbers Behind Why This Matters Now

The pet dietary supplements market is on track to hit $5.11 billion in 2026, growing at a 7.4% CAGR through 2031. Chewable and soft-chew formats now make up roughly 48% of that market — pet parents overwhelmingly prefer something that looks and tastes like a treat over anything resembling medicine. That single format shift is exactly why supplements, dental, and treats have converged into one buying occasion: they're delivered the same way and shelved the same way in a customer's head.

Meanwhile, bundling itself is producing some of the best lift numbers in ecommerce right now. Fixed bundles ("Pampered Pup Package" style) are lifting average order value by 20-75% depending on category, mix-and-match bundles add 20-50%, and well-executed cross-sell prompts can drive up to 30% of total revenue for stores that implement them properly. One pet retailer test saw a 15% jump in attach-rate purchases just from a "you might also need" pop-up at checkout. You don't need new traffic to capture this — you need better shelving of what you already sell.

Build the Bundle Around a Life Stage, Not a Category

The mistake most stores make is bundling by product type ("Supplement Bundle: Buy 3 Save 10%"). That's a discount, not a story. The trifecta works because it's built around a pet's actual life stage or condition:

  • Senior Joint Care Stack — hip & joint chew + dental chew (older dogs get dental disease at higher rates) + a gut-health topper for sensitive digestion
  • Puppy Foundations Stack — daily multivitamin + dental chew sized for teething + a probiotic treat for diet transitions
  • Anxious/Sensitive Pet Stack — calming chew + dental chew + a limited-ingredient functional treat

Brands like Finn are already doing this at the product level, pairing Hip & Joint with a Multivitamin or a Calming Aid with a daily vitamin. As a retailer, you can do the same thing across brands — which is actually your advantage, since you're not locked into one company's formulation for every slot in the stack.

Use the Lickable and Mousse Format Wave

At Global Pet Expo 2026, the format story was impossible to miss: lickable multivitamins, mousse-texture supplements, and freeze-dried "ice cream" treats with functional ingredients baked in. Zesty Paws showed an 8-in-1 Multivitamin Mousse for cats built around AlaskaOmega fish oil, probiotics, and taurine. GreenLine Pet Supply's WiggleWhip freeze-dried "dog ice cream" line uses goat milk for probiotics and gut support. Pet Krewe's Salty Cat line added a lickable Daily Multivitamin covering 12 nutrients.

These formats are built for bundling because they straddle "treat" and "supplement" — a customer doesn't think of them as medicine, so they're an easy add-on next to a dental chew or joint supplement. If you're not already stocking 1-2 lickable or mousse SKUs, this is the easiest new bundle component to add this quarter.

Merchandise It Like a Stack, Not a Sale

On your product pages and collection pages, stop showing "Supplements," "Dental," and "Treats" as three flat grids. Instead:

  • Create a dedicated "Wellness Stacks" collection with 3-4 pre-built bundles by life stage
  • On every individual supplement product page, add a "Complete the Stack" module showing the matching dental product and functional treat
  • Price the bundle at a 10-12% discount versus buying separately — enough to feel like a deal, not so much that you erode the 40-60% margins these categories typically carry
  • Use subscription pricing on the bundle itself, not just individual products — a stacked autoship is stickier than three separate ones

If you're on Shopify, native bundle apps or theme-level bundle blocks can have this live in an afternoon. You don't need a custom build to test this.

Where This Beats Chewy and Amazon

Big retailers can show you "customers also bought" — but they can't build a curated, condition-specific stack with a sentence of expert reasoning behind it the way you can. "We pair this hip & joint chew with this specific dental chew because senior dogs develop both joint and dental issues at the same time, and most owners don't realize it" is a paragraph Chewy's algorithm will never write. That's your wedge. Use it on the product page, in an email flow, and in a single TikTok video walking through the stack.

The Bottom Line

You already have the inventory for this. You almost certainly already sell supplements, dental products, and functional treats — they're just sitting in separate aisles, both physically and in your customers' minds. Pick your three best-selling SKUs across each category, build one life-stage bundle this week, and put it on a dedicated landing page with a modest discount. Measure AOV before and after. The lift is sitting there waiting for you to claim it.
